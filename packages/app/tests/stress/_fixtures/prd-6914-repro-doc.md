### Live-bait experiment

- Took the **small flounder** and rigged it as **live bait on a circle-hook rig**, hooked **through the gills**.
- **Terminal rig:** sliding-weight (Carolina-style) rig with a **3 oz** sinker attached via a **rubber band** — the rubber band lets the weight break away if it snags the structure, and the sliding setup lets a fish take line without feeling the weight before the circle hook sets.
- Idea: drop a live flatfish down near the hard structure to tempt a lingcod — lings readily eat flounder, so it's a natural bait there.

## Bottom & Structure

- **Substrate:** **Mostly sandy** across the area.
- **Hard structure:** The exceptions were the area **directly in front of the ferry** and **around the ferry pilings** — the bits of harder/structured bottom in an otherwise sandy zone.
- **Read:** Lingcod relate to rock and hard structure, so the ferry frontage and pilings are the spots most worth working for them. The surrounding sand is classic flatfish ground — which lines up with the flounder that came over the rail.

## What Worked / What Didn't

- Flounder were active and willing over the sandy bottom — 4 landed.
- Lingcod were the goal but didn't show; may need to fish tighter to the hard structure (pilings, ferry frontage) — the live-flounder-on-a-circle-hook approach is a good tool to keep trying there.
